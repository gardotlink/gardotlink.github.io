gar.link: cached onionsites on the web

What is gar.link?

gar.link (pronounced gar-dot-link, not gar-lic) is a service that exposes Tor Onionsites and Onion Services to the public World Wide Web without access to the Tor Browser Bundle, similar to tor2web and other Tor proxies.

Do not attempt to slice gar.link’s internals, as it smells, similar to slicing open an onion.

What sets gar.link apart from other Tor proxy services?

Unlike other Tor proxies, gar.link can dynamically cache requests so that similar, repeated requests do not have to go through the Tor network for some time, and supports any Internet-based protocol that runs on TCP/IP (and not just HTTP).

We cache supported Onionsites and Onion Services on a CDN so that accessing content hosted from these Onion Services are considerably faster for end users.

gar.link addresses also support being set as a CNAME record, so that you can link your own domain or subdomain to any Onion Service and serve any protocol over that linked domain.

Example use cases:

  • Securely access Onionsites from regions and organizations where Tor is censored
  • Access services like SecureDrop, the New York Times or the BBC without revealing your identity even when you temporarily do not have access to the Tor Browser Bundle
  • Host your own Git repository as a Tor Onionsite and allow contributors to push Git commits through the Web with zero additional setup
  • Host your own Mastodon instance as an Onionsite and make it accessible to non-Tor users
  • Access a remote server over Tor with SSH
  • And many, many more …

Similar with most other Tor to Web proxies, however, going through a Tor proxy still guarantees privacy of the website owner but not you, the client. Your ISP may snoop on your connection and inform relevant authorities that you are accessing gar.link services, or even which Onionsite you are trying to access through gar.link. In countries where such activities are illegal, you may be detained, jailed or face inhumane treatment.

You should have no expectation of privacy while accessing gar.link services. Should you have any concerns regarding your own privacy, safety or security, we highly recommend using the Tor Browser Bundle and accessing the Onionsite link directly instead.

How does gar.link work?

Simply type onionsite_link.gar.link on your web browser (for Onionsite onionsite_link.onion), or add a CNAME record pointing to this address. Any protocol being served over TCP/IP should work, although we did not test every single protocol being served over gar.link services.

Internally, we assign an IPv6 address for every supported Onionsite, and relay every request coming through that IPv6 address to its underlying Onion Service address through the Tor network. For cached Onionsites, we serve our locally cached copy first, and update our local cache every 10 minutes from the origin Tor Onionsite.

What is your Privacy Policy?

We do not store or log any information regarding you, the user, or the Onionsite. Period.

We also do not actively collect or produce statistics data from using our Services.

The gar.link domain, DNS records, and webhost service providers are subject to the laws of the Republic of Iceland, which has some of the strongest user privacy protections on Earth.

This does not prevent your ISP, local governments, or malicious actors from attempting to log or monitor your traffic going through gar.link. Always exercise caution before interacting with our Services.

Should you not trust us with your data, consider directly accessing the Onionsite with the Tor Browser Bundle instead. That way you do not need to trust any party, as the Tor network ensures privacy of its users by default.

How do you moderate content being served through gar.link?

The Tor network preserves privacy for both its users and websites alike, which often means horrific, unfavorable or inhumane information — terrorism, illegal arms trades, drugs, pedophiles, and those alike. We do recognize that such information may be tunneled through our services and may cause harm for our users that do not engage in such activities. We also acknowledge that this is a consequence of having zero censorship on the Tor network, which is a feature we appreciate and have built this service for in the first place.

First, we do not cache every single Onionsite that is requested through gar.link, we only cache websites that we manually verify to not serve information that is considered illicit or inhumane. A full list will be released on our Git repository. If you would like your Onionsite to be whitelisted on the cached websites list, please leave a pull request.

We also reserve the right to block access to some Onionsites on our blacklist, of which we will not release due to concerns Onion addresses for illicit Onionsites might be shared through our blacklist. However, you can check whether a particular Onionsite is blacklisted by trying access for that Onionsite through gar.link yourself (we do not log who have attempted to access a blacklisted Onionsite under our Privacy Policy).

Our policy is to only blacklist Onionsites that would be considered illegal on every single jurisdiction on Earth. If there is more than one jurisdiction that allows activities on a particular Onionsite, we will never blacklist those Onionsites. This is a baseline ethics rule we have set to provide maximum freedom for our users and Onionsite operators while avoiding content that clearly would be classified as crimes against humanity.

As an additional safety measure, any content being served through gar.link is blocked from being indexed by search engines.

Should you have concerns regarding a particular Onionsite being proxied through gar.link, please do not hesitate to email us at team@gar.link.
